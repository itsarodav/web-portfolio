If you've peeked around here, thank you! Let's switch into chat mode :). It all started with music. Before I even knew what I wanted to do, I was already obsessed with rhythm, layers, and the tiny details, plus the music industry itself. That curiosity led me to graphic design: logos for bands, cover art, and identities for creative projects (and a few social ones, too). Alongside that, I've always been a computer kid: playing, tinkering, learning, and making things. So my interest in code, and in how websites and apps are actually built, has been part of me from day one.

These days I work as a design engineer after a path through product design. What drives me is the connection between technology and people: how we use apps every day, how they integrate with devices, and what makes an experience feel smooth, accessible, simple, and consistent. Music runs in parallel as Marces (Spotify link for now, website coming soon), and I'm serious about growing that project as an independent artist.

Outside of work, I'm big on ergonomics and optimizing setups (yes, I'm the person who looks at your desk and starts diagnosing it like a patient: "your screen is too low," "that chair is crying for help"…). I also disappear into device and app reviews, especially anything living at the intersection of tech and music. I read a lot, keep doing branding just for fun, and sometimes invent projects purely to give them an identity and bring them to life in code. I share that exploration through lemonims, my personal lab and small creative label.
